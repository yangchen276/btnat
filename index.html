About Bhutan Nature


Bhutan Nature is a website created to explore the beautiful wildlife, forests, rivers, mountains, and natural landscapes of Bhutan. The website shares information about Bhutan’s rich biodiversity and unique Himalayan environment.

Bhutan is one of the few countries in the world that protects most of its forests and natural resources. Large areas of the country are covered with forests, national parks, and wildlife sanctuaries.

Visitors can learn about rare Himalayan animals such as snow leopards, takins, red pandas, black-necked cranes, and Bengal tigers. The website explains the importance of protecting endangered wildlife.

Bhutan Nature also provides information about famous rivers including Mo Chhu, Pho Chhu, Wang Chhu, and Drangme Chhu. These rivers are important for farming, hydropower, tourism, and Bhutan’s natural beauty.

The website includes details about Bhutan’s forests and national parks such as Royal Manas National Park, Jigme Dorji National Park, and Phrumsengla National Park. These forests are home to many plants, birds, and animal species.

Bhutan Nature encourages environmental conservation and eco-tourism. It helps people understand how protecting forests, rivers, and wildlife is important for future generations.

Thank you for visiting Bhutan Nature. We hope this website helps you learn more about Bhutan’s amazing environment, wildlife, and natural heritage.
